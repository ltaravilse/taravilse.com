Jury and Problemsetter at ACM ICPC

In 2012, soon after I finished my participation as a contestant in ACM ICPC, I started working as a jury and problem setter. This year I wrote 3 problems for the Argentina's Programming Tournament (TAP). Those problems were D (Designing T-Shirts), E (Emma's Domino) and I (In Debt).

In 2013 I also wrote a problem for the TAP, problem B (Elections). I also wrote problems F (Football) and I (Inverting Huffman) for the Latin American Regional contest.

In 2014 I wrote problems G (Gallantry) and J (Distracted Judges) for the TAP and problem D (Dividing the names) for the Latin American Regional contest.

After leaving from the jury for coaching teams in 2015, I returned in 2016 to the problem setting and jury tasks with problems E (Grumpy Uncle), F (No English translation yet) and J (Joining Lines).

During all this years I was member of the jury in the Buenos Aires site of the TAP and the Latin American Regionals.
